What TSW Is
A basketball stat-tracking and league management platform for coaches, team managers, and league organizers. Users track live games using a court-tap interface, derive box scores and shot maps from events, manage rosters, run leagues with standings and join requests, and share highlights/stats through a public social feed.

Who This Should Be Sold To
Based on the feature set, there are three distinct audiences:

Audience Who they are What they use it for
Recreational / amateur coaches A coach of a local rec league team, school team, or pickup squad Track their own team's games, share stats with players, build a public presence
League administrators/organizers Someone who runs a recreational basketball league (5-20 teams) Manage the full league: standings, team rosters, game scheduling, public-facing pages for all teams
Semi-pro / competitive club teams Club teams, AAU programs, academy teams Serious stat review — shot maps, replay analysis, season trends, public player pages for recruiting exposure
Pricing Recommendation
The current model ($0 free / $8/mo per team) is under-differentiated — it only gates two features behind Pro, and leagues (which are high-value and complex) are essentially free. Here's a rethink across your three audiences:

Tier 1: Free — "Tracker"
Target: Casual coaches, solo team managers, try-before-you-buy

Includes:

1 team
Unlimited game tracking
Box score, recap tab, play-by-play
Public team + player pages
Feed posts (image, game/player/team cards)
1 league (as a team member — not as organizer)
Excludes: Replay, shot maps, league creation/management, multiple teams

Tier 2: Team Pro — "Coach" — $9–12/mo per team or $79–99/season per team
Target: Coaches who want deeper game review for one team

Adds to Free:

Replay tab (event-by-event)
Shot maps (public + private)
Highlight clip sharing
Unlimited teams (or just the one upgraded)
"Per season" pricing works well here since recreational leagues run in defined seasons. Give them the choice: pay monthly or save 20% with a season upfront.

Tier 3: League Organizer — $29–49/mo or $199–299/season per league
Target: League admins running 4–20+ teams in a structured competition

Includes everything in Team Pro, plus:

League creation + management
Up to N teams in the league (e.g. 10 included, +$5/team after)
Dual-team tracking for league games
Public standings/league pages
Join request system
Multiple league managers
League logo + branding
This is where the real revenue is. Someone running a 12-team rec league is getting enormous value — standings automation, public pages for every team, player profiles. Currently that's free, which is leaving money on the table.

Optional: Player/Fan tier — Free (with optional "Claimed Profile")
Players can claim their roster slot and see their own stats. This is already built. You could upsell a cosmetic "Claimed Profile" badge or keep it permanently free to drive organic word-of-mouth (players share their stat page with friends).

Summary Recommendation
The biggest change I'd suggest: charge for leagues, not just per-team Pro. The league organizer is your highest-value user — they bring 4–20 teams onto the platform in one go. Right now they pay $0.

A clean structure:

Free: 1 team, tracking + box scores, public pages, feed
Team Pro (~$10/mo or ~$80/season): replay + shot maps per team, for coaches who want deeper review
League (~$40/mo or ~$250/season): full league management for organizers, includes Pro features for all league-managed teams in that league
