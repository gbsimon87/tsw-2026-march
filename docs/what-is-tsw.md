What TSW Is
A basketball stat-tracking and league management platform for coaches, team managers, and league organizers. Users track live games using a court-tap interface, derive box scores and shot maps from events, manage rosters, run leagues with standings and join requests, and share highlights/stats through a public social feed.

Complete Feature Inventory
Core (available to everyone, free)
Account registration (email + Google OAuth)
Team & roster creation (name, colors, logo, player positions/numbers)
Live game tracking (court-tap shot capture, FTs, assists, rebounds, steals, blocks, turnovers, fouls, subs)
Lineup management (starting 5, subs during game)
Opponent score tracking (totals)
Game finish + box score derivation
Game history list
Sortable stats tab + play-by-play log on game detail
Public team pages (season stats table, player roster, upcoming/recent games)
Public player pages (per-game logs, PPG/RPG/APG)
Public social feed ("The Pulse") — image posts, game/player/team shareable cards, highlight clips
YouTube video link per game (for low-cost playback)
Print mode for game detail (?print=1)
Game recap tab (top performers, key moments, shareable recap card)
League Management (free, but gated by an entitlement field per user — "league creation")
Create and run leagues (slug-based, with logo)
League standings (W-L, PF, PA, differential)
Dual-team game tracking (both teams tracked simultaneously in league games)
League team roster + player management
Player join requests (approve/reject flow)
Team roles: Manager, Helper, Player
Public league pages (standings, games, team pages, player profiles)
League managers (multi-admin support)
"My Sporty" page — player's league profile overview
Pro features (Team Pro: $12/mo or $89/season per team; League Pro: $49/mo or $299/season per league)
Replay tab — event-by-event progression with live-updating box score
Public shot maps — visual shot location charts on public game pages
Priority billing support (stated, not technically enforced)
Who This Should Be Sold To
Based on the feature set, there are three distinct audiences:

Audience Who they are What they use it for
Recreational / amateur coaches A coach of a local rec league team, school team, or pickup squad Track their own team's games, share stats with players, build a public presence
League administrators/organizers Someone who runs a recreational basketball league (5-20 teams) Manage the full league: standings, team rosters, game scheduling, public-facing pages for all teams
Semi-pro / competitive club teams Club teams, AAU programs, academy teams Serious stat review — shot maps, replay analysis, season trends, public player pages for recruiting exposure
